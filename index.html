Wind Energy 

Wind energy is based on the transformation of wind energy into useful energy. It is a renewable and clean energy source as it does not emit polluting gases into the atmosphere.
Mechanical wind energy is transformed into electrical energy through equipment called a wind turbine, which includes propellers that move at wind speed.

This power generation system works due to the blades that absorb kinetic energy from wind force that in turn rotates the blades, which move a shaft,converting it into mechanical energy that drives a generator producing electricity. The generator basically consists of a magnet and a coil that, when rotating one another, produce electric current. The current produced will be directed to a transformation station and finally distributed by the electricity grid. 

A wind farm can be used in three different systems, each of these systems as differents purposes and energy potential. An isolated system is a system that is deprived of electricity from the public grid, being used only to supply certain regions. A hybrid system produces electricity simultaneously with more than one source of energy, could it be photovoltaic panels or wind turbines. The last one it's the Grid-connected system. These systems are responsible to insert the energy they produce into the public grid. Offshore windturbine support structures and configurations

The current production of offshore wind energy requires more efficient solutions, leading to the wind towers are deployed away from the coast, where wind speeds increase substantially with height since the land topography is more uniform and there are no major obstacles to the wind.




Gravity Base Foundation

The gravity foundation works by using its own weight to stabilize the wind, wave, and ice forces that act on the tower.
A steel or concrete box is dipped to the bottom of the sea and is filled with stones, concrete or other high-density material. This type of foundation is vulnerable to erosion at the base that is caused by water currents. Therefore, further preparation of the seabed is necessary, creating a horizontal surface with greater resistance to the effect of currents. 

The main advantages of this type of foundation are:
• Simpler removal (not fixed to the seabed);
• Good corrosion resistance of concrete;
• More economical construction.


The main disadvantages of this type of foundation are:
• Need for prior preparation of the installation site;
• Expensive installation;
• Need for various equipment for installation and transportation;
• Impact on the seabed. 





Monopile Structure

The technique is relatively simple and usually does not require any ground pretreatment. However, during installation, equipment with high lifting capacity is required.
The monopile foundation can be used in a variety of bed conditions, such as mixtures of stones, sand or clay. As long as the foundation is deployed at a depth of 10 to 40 meters on the bottom substrate there will be no problems in areas with large sediment movements.
Compared to gravity foundations, monopiles have less environmental impact. 

The main advantages of this type of foundation are:
• Easy of manufacture;
• Easy of transport;
• Lower cost than other alternatives;
• It does not require seabed preparation.

The main disadvantages of this type of foundation are:
• Need for protection against erosion;
• The environmental impact caused during its installation;
• Inability to apply to larger turbines;
• Not applicable at high depths. 





Tripod Structure

A tripod foundation consists of a monopile divided into the base in a triangular shape. The connection to ground is made by tubes of smaller diameter compared to a monopile foundation. One of the greatest advantages of a tripod foundation in relation to gravity and monopile foundation is that it can be deployed in deeper zones. In a depth range of 20 to 40 meters the tripod foundation can have a technical and economic advantage.

The main advantages of this type of foundation are:
• Stability and Resistance;
• Applicable to greater depths and larger wind turbines;
• Construction of Maritime Wind Farms: Processes and Work Direction;
• Low problems with erosion.

The main disadvantages of this type of foundation are:
• Complexity and cost of manufacture;
• Logistic complexity in dealing with large quantities;
• Need to make a connection between the foundation and the piles (“grouting”). 

Jacket Structure

Jacket foundations are standard in the oil and gas industry and have been adapted for offshore turbines. It is a tower anchored at the bottom of the sea and is usually composed of four main legs and secondary bars that are connected by welding or molded sleeves that are assembled prior to transportation to the site.
A transition piece is mounted between the tower and the foundation to ensure proper force transfer.

The main advantages of this type of foundation are:
• High resistance to moments;
• Applicable to depths greater than those allowed by gravity or pile foundations only;
• Rigid and stable support structure.

The main disadvantages of this type of foundation are:
• Complex fabrication structure due to the high number of nodes to be welded;
• Not suitable for ice zones as the area at the waterline level is high. 

Windfloat Structure

Windfloat is a pioneer project worldwide whose main objective is the exploration of the deepwater wind resource.

This technology allows the exploration of offshore wind potential at depths greater than 40 meters. The focus of the project's innovation is based on the development of a semi-submersible and triangular floating platform, originating from the oil and gas extraction industry, which houses a wind turbine with several MW of production capacity.

The floating platform is anchored to the seabed and its stability is achieved through a water-filled gate system at the base of the three pillars, coupled with a static and dynamic ballast system.
These platforms are designed for risk reduction, both in the construction phase, as well as in the installation and operation phase.

These platforms are designed for risk reduction, both in the construction phase, as well as in the installation and operation phase. Thus, WindFloat technology is independent of wind turbine used and of the site and installation depth, it has stability due to water ballast and stabilizing plates at the base of the columns. And the construction is made entirely on land, including turbine mounting, leading to a simplified installation process, thus avoiding difficult and costly offshore work.

Offshore Wind Energy in Portugal

In recent times it has been found that the higher altitude areas in Portugal are already overloaded with wind farms, consequently, the exploration of offshore wind farms has given new opportunities to wind energy.

Wind energy potential is used globally as a clean and green energy resource for the production of electricity. In Portugal, since 2000, wind technology has achieved remarkable growth, making the country one of the main drivers of this sector at the European level. This was due to incentives from European and National policy on endogenous and renewable resources aimed at diversifying sources, improving the security of supply, reducing energy dependency and reducing the environmental impact of the power system.

Targets have been set for renewable energy generation by the European Parliament and the EU Council. These targets state that by the year 2030, 35% of the energy produced in Portugal must come from renewable sources. Currently, there are about 250 active wind farms that correspond to an installed capacity of 5.3 GW, about 19%, corresponding to 339.52MW. between 15 and 20 years. This factor urges that the national wind sector is aging and that this trend tends to prosper. 


Although there are other factors that make it possible to operate after 20 years, there is no legislation that guarantees the extension of wind turbine life safely for all wind farm operators, which makes their maintenance difficult. Looking at the short term, the extension of the wind turbine's lifetime will not be permanent, and by 2023 about 72% of wind farms in Portugal will be over 15 years old. In a situation where no new investments occur, it is expected that the national wind sector will decline radically over the next few years, diverging from the imposed targets. 


The saturation of the National Territory in view of its availability for onshore wind energy has encouraged Portugal to become a pioneer in Offshore Wind Energy.
The WindFloat Atlantic Project
The WindFloat Atlantic Project developed an innovative technology to the exploitation of wind potential at sea, at depths of more than 40m. The innovation focus of the project was the conception of a floating foundation technology, to support multi-MW wind turbines in offshore applications.  This wind farm consists of three 25 MW wind turbines with 8.4 MW each. They are placed on floating platforms, anchored to a depth of 100 meters and located 20 kilometers off the coast of Viana de Castelo. This project has the capacity to supply the electrical needs of 60 thousand people.An investment of 125 million euros, WindFloat Atlantic has received financial support from the European Commission, the European Investment Bank and the Portuguese government.
